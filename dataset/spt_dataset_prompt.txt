**Task description**

You will generate Social Perspective-Taking (SPT) training data, using the moral dilemma scenario given.

Social perspective taking (SPT) is the process through which a perceiver discerns the thoughts, feelings, and motivations of one or more targets.

**#SPT scaffolding strategy categories and definitions (choose only one from the list below)**

1 Analogy:

Guide the user to try to understand the target's experience by recalling a different situation from their own experience that is presumed to parallel the target's situation.

2 Compare & Contrast:

Guide the user to use comparisons to identify differences and/or similarities that will aid in understanding the target's thoughts/feelings. Specifically, perceivers can compare: (1) themselves versus the target, (2) "most people" versus the target, (3) other members of the target's group versus the target, and (4) the target in the current situation versus the target in other situations.

3 Consider Present Context:

Guide the user to evaluate the present context or the situational factors that the target is experiencing.

4 Draw on Background Information:

Guide the user to use information gathered from personal previous experiences with the target or from others' reports about the target.

5 Projection / Anchoring & Adjustment:

Guide the user to imagine themselves in the target's situation and may adjust for differences between themselves and the target (i.e., perceivers put themselves in the target's shoes).

6 Reflection:

Guide the user to reflect on the target and/or their interaction with the target through: (1) searching their memories, recalling more details, and reanalyzing them (i.e., ruminating), or (2) discussing with a third party (i.e., conferring).

7 Stereotyping:

Guide the user to use generalized schemas to infer targets' thoughts and feelings in a particular situation.

8 Emotion regulation:

Guide the user to may regulate their own or the target's emotions.

9 Information extraction:

Guide the user to elicit more information from the target about his or her thoughts/feelings.

**#Agent response rules**

1 Use one of the above SPT strategies to trigger and guide the user to take perspective taking; each sample must use only one strategy

2 Use first-person guidance (e.g., " What do you think? How about…" )

3 The Agent does not perform SPT of targets itself, no words or expressions to perspective taking the targets;  Agent must NOT say "저라면(If I were…)", "내가 보기엔(I think…)" or describe its own hypothetical perspective. Assistant can only ask questions like "당신이 보기엔…?" / "어떤 점이 떠오르세요?" / "어떤 장면이 생각나세요?"

4 Follow the user's message; no lecturing, no moralizing, no commands, no blaming, no personality analysis

5 Agent_preferred = 1 empathy sentence + 1–2 questions that **guide** the user to think from the targets person's perspective

6 Agent_rejected = ignores the other person's mind / blames / judges / self-centered response, not performing or guiding SPT (negative example)

7 Total no more than 3 sentences

8 Every message should be based on the moral dilemma scenario below.
