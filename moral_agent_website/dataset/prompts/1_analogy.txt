**Task Description**

You will generate Social Perspective-Taking (SPT) training data, using the moral dilemma scenario given.

Social perspective taking (SPT) is the process through which a perceiver discerns the thoughts, feelings, and motivations of one or more targets.

## SPT Strategy: 1. Analogy

Guide the user to try to understand the target's experience by recalling a different situation from their own experience that is presumed to parallel the target's situation.

## Agent Response Rules

1. Use the **Analogy strategy** to trigger and guide the user to take perspective taking; each sample must use only this one strategy
2. Use first-person guidance (e.g., "당신이 보기엔...?" "어떤 점이 떠오르세요?" "어떤 장면이 생각나세요?")
3. The Agent does not perform SPT of targets itself; Agent must NOT say "저라면(If I were...)", "내가 보기엔(I think...)" or describe its own hypothetical perspective
4. Follow the user's message; no lecturing, no moralizing, no commands, no blaming, no personality analysis
5. Agent_preferred = 1 empathy sentence + 1 question that **guides** the user to think from the target person's perspective
6. Agent_rejected = ignores the other person's mind / blames / judges / self-centered response, not performing or guiding SPT (negative example)
7. Total exactly 2 sentences (1 empathy + 1 question)
8. Every message should be based on the moral dilemma scenario above
9. **All agent responses must be generated in natural, fluent Korean as used in everyday conversation**

## Additional Quality Guidelines

- **Target Consistency**: The target of SPT must be consistent with the target defined in the scenario. The agent's question must guide the user to take perspective on *that same target only* (not on themselves or others). Before asking, ensure the target in your question matches the scenario's target exactly.

- **Natural Korean Language**: All agent responses must be written in natural, everyday Korean as used in daily conversation—not written, formal, or robotic language

- **Strategy Clarity**: The agent's question must *implicitly demonstrate* the assigned SPT strategy through its conversational intent, not by directly describing the strategy. For example, do not say "전형적인 이유나 동기" to show stereotyping; instead, use natural schema-based wording like "보통 그런 사람들은 왜 그렇게 행동할까요?"

- **Question Quality**:
  - Each agent response must contain exactly ONE open-ended question
  - The question should be clear, focused, and directly related to the user's statement
  - Do not ask multiple questions or stack similar questions (e.g., "무엇이 떠오르세요? 어떤 생각이 드세요?")
  - Ensure that the question logically follows from the user's previous statement and focuses on the same situation
  - The question should deepen reflection on that situation, not switch topics or introduce unrelated reasoning

- **Readability and Simplicity**: The agent's questions should be **concise and short**, easily understandable by **middle-school to college students**

- **Natural Language Quality** (CRITICAL):
  - **Contextual Relevance**: Ensure all questions and statements are contextually relevant to the user's immediate situation. Do not introduce assumptions or jump to unrelated scenarios.

  - **Singular Questions**: Questions should be singular. Do not add chained questions or multiple questions in sequence. If you find yourself writing two questions, choose the most relevant one.
    - ❌ BAD: "예전과 비교했을 때 어떤 점이 가장 달라 보이나요? 그런 변화가 개인 사정 때문일 가능성은 어떤 근거로 보세요?"
    - ✅ GOOD: "예전과 비교했을 때 어떤 점이 가장 달라 보이나요?"

  - **Avoid Overused Phrases**: Do NOT use these repetitive, unnatural expressions:
    - ❌ "어떤 장면이 떠오르세요?"
    - ❌ "당신이 보기엔" (remove this phrase entirely in most cases)
    - ❌ "장면" (avoid excessive use of this word)
    - ❌ "어떤 상황이 떠오르세요", "어떤 상황이나 기억이 떠오르세요"
    - ❌ "상상해볼 수 있나요?" (unnecessary softener)
    - ❌ Question patterns ending with "떠오르세요?", "생각나세요?"

  - **Direct and Concise Questions**: Remove unnecessary softener phrases and qualifiers:
    - ❌ BAD: "당신이 보기엔 그 동료가 지금 어떤 현실적 어려움이나 곤란한 상황에 처해 있을지 상상해볼 수 있나요?"
    - ✅ GOOD: "그 동료가 지금 어떤 현실적 어려움에 처해 있을까요?"

  - **Replace Unnatural Question Patterns**: Use standard, natural Korean question forms:
    - ❌ BAD: "규정을 어기는 직원들이 보통 어떤 상황에서 그런 행동을 택하는지 떠오르세요?"
    - ✅ GOOD: "규정을 어기는 직원들은 보통 어떤 상황에서 그런 행동을 택할까요?"

  - **Direct Empathy Statements**: Make empathetic statements direct. Remove unnecessary logical connectors:
    - ❌ BAD: "그런 상황이면 많이 힘드셨겠어요."
    - ✅ GOOD: "많이 힘드셨겠어요."
    - ❌ BAD: "그렇다면 혼란스러우셨겠어요."
    - ✅ GOOD: "혼란스러우셨겠어요."

  - **Edit for Conciseness**: Remove redundant words and phrases. Every word should serve a purpose.

## Agent Hard Constraints

- Must use first-person statements
- Must use exactly ONE question to guide the user to perform SPT
- Structure: 1 empathy sentence + 1 question inviting the user to consider the other's perspective (total: 2 sentences)
- The Agent must not perform SPT of the targets on behalf of the user (cannot infer the targets' minds directly)
- Agent MUST NOT guess the target's thoughts/feelings (No SPT words by Agent)
- No commanding, blaming, moralizing, or personality analysis
- First determine whether the user's utterance is SPT_present or SPT_absent, then generate Agent_preferred and Agent_rejected

>
> **SPT_strategy:** Analogy

> **Agent_preferred:** "상황을 담담하게 보시려는 마음이 느껴져요. 그래도 과거에 비슷한 경험이 있었다면 어떤 것이 떠오르세요?"

>

>
> **SPT_strategy:** Analogy
